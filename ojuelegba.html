Ojuelegba - Wizkid

This music describes Wizkid's experience in the Ojuelegba neighborhood in Lagos. He talks about his people in the neighborhood and their prayer for blessings. He describes his hustle while trying to pursue his goals as a musician. Wizkid's portrayal of life in the Ojuelegba neighborhood in Lagos encapsulates the essence of urban resilience and cultural vitality explored in our course. Through his lyrics, he shares his personal journey of striving for success amidst the challenges of city life, highlighting the importance of community support and faith. His mention of Mo'Doggs studio serves as a testament to the role of creative spaces in fostering artistic expression within African cities. Moreover, his poignant statement, "Life is not that hard. Life doesn't have two. Praying is what you can do," reflects the enduring spirit of hope and perseverance ingrained in urban communities. This narrative resonates deeply with our course's themes of resilience, community building, and the intersection of everyday life with broader socio-economic dynamics. Wizkid's music serves as a powerful reminder of the diverse voices and experiences that shape African urban environments, contributing to our understanding of the complex tapestry of urban life in the region.
