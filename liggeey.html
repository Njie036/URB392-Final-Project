Liggeey - Youssou Ndour

"Liggeey" by Youssou Ndour, featured in his album "Joko: From Village to Town," released on February 1st, 2000, emphasizes the significance of work or "liggeey" in achieving success. The song underscores the importance of labor and earning one's own income, particularly relevant in the context of African cities undergoing rapid urbanization. Ndour's opening line, "Teranga dormou adama, liggeey dong moko muna jokheh” which translates to “work is the only way that one can achieve successs”. This highlights that work is the essential path to success. This resonates with the theme of economic empowerment and self-reliance, central to understanding the socio-economic dynamics of African urban environments. The song's message extends beyond mere financial gain, advocating for the dignity of labor and the shared responsibility of both men and women to contribute to society through their work. By promoting the value of hard work and self-sufficiency, "Liggeey" addresses pertinent themes of labor, gender roles, and economic agency, offering insights into the challenges and aspirations of individuals navigating urban life in Africa.
