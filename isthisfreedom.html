Is this Freedom - Lucky Dube

In this song, Dube offers a critical reflection on the post-apartheid era in South Africa, questioning whether the newfound political freedom has truly translated into tangible improvements in the lives of ordinary people, especially those who have suffered under decades of oppression. Through the perspective of an elderly woman who has endured the hardships of apartheid, Dube highlights the skepticism and disillusionment that many South Africans felt in the aftermath of the political transition. The woman's concerns about the concentration of power in the hands of a single individual, as expressed in lines like “She looks one look at the man; Throws her hands in the air; And said God help us all; Too much power in one mans hands; Is dangerous; She has never tasted freedom” - reflect broader anxieties about the potential for corruption, injustice, and continued inequality in the new democratic order. She has a sense of disillusionment and skepticism towards the new government, suggesting that power has merely transferred from "one fool and liar to another," raising doubts about real change. Dube's lyrics poignantly convey the woman's sense of disappointment and betrayal, as she witnesses the perpetuation of old patterns of oppression and exploitation under the guise of freedom. By questioning whether real change has occurred or if power has simply shifted from one oppressive regime to another, Dube exposes the complexities and challenges of the post-colonial struggle for liberation and justice in South Africa and beyond.
